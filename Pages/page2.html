Top Ten Animated Movies

10 - The Hobbit

This is an animated TV special based on The Hobbit by J.R.R. Tolkien. It's definitely not a masterpiece, considering the designs are a bit weird-looking most of the time, and the animation is a bit limited due to their budget. Though, despite this, it does have some charm, and it's a bit entertaining to watch, especially as it is a pretty accurate adaptation for the most part. Here is a link to the IMDB page.

9 - Wall-E

Wall-E is an animated film by Pixar that was released in 2008. The premise involves a robot named Wall-E who is on Earth thousands of years in the future, where the entire earth is covered in garbage. Wall-E discovers a plant, which seems to be the last one on the earth, which he keeps, and he meets a robot named Eve who is looking for the plant. This film has great animation that tsill holds up today, and it has a heartwarming story as well. You can check out the IMDB page for more information.

8 - Mulan

Mulan is an animated film by Disney that was released in 1998. It is adapted from a Chinese ballad about a girl who is worried her father will die when he is drafted into war, and disguises as a man to take his place. The film's animation is great, and the characters are interesting. The film also has a darker tone near the second half, which is emphasized by the lack of songs after the film's first half. If you're interested, you can find more information on IMDB.

7 - Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse

Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse is an animated film released in 2018, and was based on multiple versions of the iconic superhero Spider-Man. The main protagonist of the film is Miles Morales, who gets bitten by a radioactive spider, and ends up meeting multiple different versions of Spider-Man who help train him to become Spider-Man himself. The film has a very unique style, almost looking like a comic book in motion. The characters are also written well, especially Miles, who is an interesting character and has character growth throughout the course of the film. There's more information about this film on its IMDB page.

6 - Incredibles

The Incredibles is an animated film that was released in 2004. It centers on a family of superheroes in a world where superheroes have been outlawed due to various controversies in the past. The film's animation is noticeably a bit older compared to what films have been released more recently, though the character designs and the visual style of the film still hold up pretty well. The story is also pretty strong, and the characters are written in a believeable way. Here's the IMDB page for this film, if you're interested in reading more about it.

5 - Up

Up is an animated film that was released in 2009. It is about an elderly man named Carl Fredrickson who uses a bunch of balloons to lift his house and visit a place in South America called Paradise Falls, as he had promised his late wife Ellie that they would visit there. The film's intro is very emotional, as it shows Carl and Ellie meeting for the first time, and goes on a montage of them living out their lives together, with Carl eventually being alone after Ellie passes on. The film also has good animation and character designs, which have aged well. If you want to read more, you can check out the IMDB page.

4 - Batman: Mask of the Phantasm

Batman: Mask of the Phantasm is an animated film which spun-off from Batman: The Animated Series. The plot revolves around Batman trying to stop a cloaked villain who has been killing people around Gotham City, who many assume is Batman himself. The film's animation is an improved version of the series' animation due to it having a higher budget, and it is also a bit darker than the main series since it was a theatrical release instead of being aired on television. It also has a well-written story as well. If you want to read more, you can check out the IMDB page.

3 - Atlantis: The Lost Empire

Atlantis: The Lost Empire is an animated film by Disney that was released in 2001. It is about a man named Milo Thatch who ends up being a part of a crew that goes to search for the lost city of Atlantis. The film has a very nice art style, and the characters were designed by Mike Mignola, the creator of the Hellboy comics. It also has an interesting story, though it didn't do as well when it first came out due to it being more adventure-oriented compared to most other Disney films at the time. You can read more about this film on IMDB if you're interested.

2 - The Prince of Egypt

The Prince of Egypt is an animated film by Dreamworks that was released in 1998. It adapts the biblical story of Moses, though it also takes liberties with the source material to tell an interesting narrative, which it achieves. Being a musical, it has songs in it, and the songs add to the story really well. The character designs are also well-done, and the art style looks great! Here's a link to this film's IMDB page, if you're interested in seeing more about it.

1 - Klaus

Klaus is a holiday movie that was released on Netflix in 2019. It is written as an origin story for Santa Claus, where a selfish postman befriends an elderly toymaker named Klaus, and the two of them agree to give toys to the children in a nearby town as gifts. The film's animation is amazing and unique, as the director wanted to make a hand-drawn animated film where the actual animation had the same amount of detail as the poster and promotional art for the film would. If you're interested in learning more about the film, you can visit its IMDB page to read more about it.
